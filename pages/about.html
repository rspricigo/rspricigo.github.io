About me

After working in IT support in hospitals and providing consulting services, I spent 10 years in a hospital environment. Four of those years were as a manager, where I developed a strong foundation in healthcare operations. Seeking new challenges and driven by my long-time passion for programming, I decided to transition into system development, using my expertise in Electronic Medical Records (EMR) and healthcare integration to bring innovative solutions to the field.

My experience in healthcare plays a significant role in my work today. Having spent years supporting and even implementing hospital management systems, I understand the challenges faced by healthcare teams using these systems. I also have a strong grasp of hospital IT infrastructure, business rules across various hospital departments, and an in-depth understanding of the EMR system itself, including its database structure and functionalities.

Making the transition to programming has been challenging, especially since my formal education didn’t involve much hands-on programming time. The rapid evolution of tools and frameworks over the years has made it a challenge to catch up and stay current with the latest technologies.

What drives me most about systems integration is the potential to create solutions that genuinely benefit healthcare professionals, making their workflows easier and more efficient. By providing valuable integrations, I aim to improve service delivery and add real value to healthcare organizations.

I’m focused on achieving tangible outcomes through my work in development and integration, with a goal of reducing costs, minimizing repetitive tasks, and allowing healthcare professionals to focus more on patient care. Additionally, I strive to improve the quality and speed of information flow, ensuring more accurate metrics for decision-making.
